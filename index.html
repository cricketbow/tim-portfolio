I started using Adobe Photoshop when it was only at version 2.5.

I’ve been using Apple computers since the 1980’s (yikes!). I played around with Photoshop 2.5 when I was in college. It didn't even have layers back then. That was bad. Layers make everything easier for everyone. I like them.

I can draw and paint stuff… Even without a computer if you want me to!

I like using stock photos and illustrations as much as the next designer. However, if time allows I love making my own icons and artwork. Computers are cool, but nothing beats a good pencil! Staedtler makes a good pencil.

I can code stuff, too... I don’t like it as much as drawing, but it’s cool.

Making websites is fun, especially when the right people are involved. I know HTML and CSS and NASA and PBR and a bunch of other acronyms. Sometimes I even like to make up my own acronyms — when I’m not busy making chili…

I make good chili! It’s not super-awesome, but it’s pretty yummy.

The secret is cooking in a crock pot and adding a block of dark chocolate to enrich both the flavor and the color. Mmm-mmm… going to go make some right now.

Continuing Education

I love learning new things and I do all the time. I went to college for Industrial Design, but fell into an illustration and graphic design career upon graduation.

You’ll notice FileMaker Pro software listed under my Skills. I taught myself how to build FileMaker Pro databases a long time ago because I wanted something to track my car maintenance and to organize website accounts for myself and freelance clients. I know… pretty nerdy, right? It’s been a while since I’ve needed to use it, though, so it’s a little rusty, as there are now easier apps designed for the purpose of organizing information.

I taught myself how to build websites back in the late 90’s because I wanted to make my own online portfolio. It looked horrible, but I’ve learned a lot since then. The web is constantly evolving and it can be hard to keep up. This page is built with a Mac app called Blocs that I learned how to use a couple years ago. It’s pretty decent for making small, quick websites or landing pages like this one. I usually like to concentrate on the design aspect and leave the coding to the experts, but I’ll play around when I can or help out if the rest of the team is busy… That is, when I’m not making chili.

Obscure Fun Fact About Me

Buffy the Vampire Slayer was (and still is) my favorite TV show, so it was pretty awesome and quite surprising when I was watching episode 2 of season 4 way back in 1999 and saw a notepad I designed when I worked at Colorbök Paper Products featured in the show.
